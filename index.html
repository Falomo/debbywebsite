DRUG STUDY- RESEARCH ASSISTANT

During my fourth year, I worked with the KALE LAB (Babcock University) and worked on several pharmacology studies. See Sample Abstract below

Nephrotoxicity takes several different forms, where some drugs may present symptoms, but not impair renal functions, whereas, other drugs will elicit the exact opposite effect, possibly in more than one specific location. In addition, some widely employed medications such as antibiotics, with low potential for inducing renal disease, will possibly produce significant detrimental damage upon the kidneys of other patients. Medications and treatments, proven to produce such results, are known as “Nephrotoxic agents”. These agents consist of each of the major classes of clinically useful antibiotics, including amphotericin, polymyxins, aminoglycosides, sulfonamides, rifampicin, & penicillin, and anti-cancer treatments, such as 5-flurouracil anti-dimer. This study was carried out to investigate the modulatory effect of silymarin on amphotericin B induced nephrotoxicity by exposing 42 adult male wistar rats separated into six groups to the therapeutic, hepato-protective drug, sylimarin for a period of seven days, at the concentrations of 0.5 mg/kg, 1 mg/kg, & 2mg/kg. After which, the test subjects were exposed to the nephrotoxic agent, amphotericin-B, at a concentration of 2.5 mg/kg. The blood and kidneys of the subjects were harvested and biochemical and histopathlogical parameters were analyzed and assessed. According to the histopathological analysis, the kidneys of the Control, SYLM (2 mg/kg), AMPT-B + SYLM (1 mg/kg) and AMPT- B + SYLM (2 mg/kg) presented relatively normal results with minimal damage due to the introduction of sylimarin as the therapeutic agent. Results also showed that when amphotericin-B was compared to that of amphotericin-B + Silymarin (group A), there was a 9.92% increase (p<0.05) in the concentration of amphotericin-B, possibly indicating a hepato-protective ability of silymarin. When amphotericin-B was compared to that of amphotericin-B + silymarin (group C), which is a double therapeutic dose, amphotericin-B indicated a 16.85% increase (p<0.05) in alanine aminotransferase concentration, also signifying that, at higher concentrations, silymarin is shown to be effective in the liver.
